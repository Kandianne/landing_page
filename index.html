What is TechEquity?

There is a digital equity crisis in the Oakland and the East Bay and students from under-resourced neighborhoods, often Black or Latino, suffer the most from the resulting opportunity divide.

TechEquity is the bridge across the digital divide. We can help make it easier to cross by providing free or cheap internet and computers, mentorship and internship opportunities and education programs to those who need it. There are plenty of ways to get involved and there's no time like not to start.

We're an open source community

We're putting together a TechEquity Blueprint and a Charter to encourage Oakland employers to hire from their local community, to meet fair diveristy standards, to spend and contract locally, and to offer mentorship and volunteer opportunities.

It's all open source. If you have something to contribute, join the community and send us a pull request. We'd love your help.

Who are we?

We're a collective of folks from technology companies, technology education organizations and local community organizations. We live and/or work in Oakland, we love Oakland, and we want Oakland to stay Oakland.

What brings us together is the mission of building an inclusive, representative and community-oriented tech ecosystem in Oakland. We advocate for sustainable, equitable growth in Oakland and for policies that prevent displacement.
